I'm a Biomedical Engineering grad who somehow ended up deep in local AI and web dev. My degree is in BME, but I've always been the kind of person who learns by just building something and figuring it out along the way — which apparently applies to programming too.

I don't have a software background. What I do have is a habit of getting curious about something, going too far down the rabbit hole, and coming out the other side with a working project and a lot of opinions about it.

## 📖 How I Work

I pick up new things by building projects I actually care about. Most of my programming knowledge came from hitting a wall, spending way too long on Stack Overflow, and eventually getting it to work. That loop has taught me more than any course has.

Right now I'm focused on local AI — specifically, how much you can get out of a small model when you give it the right context. Turns out, quite a lot.
